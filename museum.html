Limerick Museum

This museum was founded in 1907. It was once known as the Jim Kemmy Municipal Museum named after Jim Kemmy who was a Labour and Democratic Socailist Party TD. It is the oldest local authority museum in limerick. It houses approximately 60,000 artifacts. It displays history from Limerick herritage. The relics presented spanning back to the Stone Age and to medieval relics. Notable pieces displayed there are the civic sword of Queen Elizabeth and a fragment of the Dromore Meteorite which is the largest meteorite in Ireland.


Admission: Free

Limerick City Gallery of Art

This Galley was founded in 1936. The building was originaly constructed as a Carnegi museum and gallery in 1906. This galley houses Irish art from the 18th to the 20th centuary. Its colleciton includes 831 pieces of historic and contempory pieces of art.In recent years the gallery is also home to The Michael O'Connor International Poster Collection which is a collection of 2,800 international and cultural posters. Noteworthy art displayed in the museum is from Jack B. Yeats who is one of the biggest 20th centuary artist, Paul Henry who was known for his painting of Irish landscapes and Grace Henry who is a historically important artist known for painting vibrant landscapes and self portrates who was a woman artist in a male dominated field.

Admission: Free
