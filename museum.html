Museums and Galleries

Limerick's Museums and galleries serve as a hub where people can witness the diverse culture, herritage and artistic talemts. It provides a way to educate the community by also enhancing community enaguement and are a pivital part of tourism. These are a vital part of Limerick's identity and a reminder of the growth of Limerick city to bring it to where it is today.

Limerick Museum

This museum was founded in 1907. It was once known as the Jim Kemmy Municipal Museum named after Kim Kemmy who was a Labour and Demoocratic Socailist Party TD. Entry is free. It is the oldest local authority museum in limerick. It houses approximately 60,000 artifacts. It displays history from Limerick herritage. The relics presented spanning back to the Stone Age and to medieval relics. Notable pieces displayed there are the civic sword of Queen Elizabeth and a fragment of the Dromore Meteorite which is the largest meteorite in Ireland.
